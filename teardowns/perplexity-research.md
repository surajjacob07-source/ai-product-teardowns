# Perplexity for Research Workflows Teardown

## 1. Summary
- Product: Perplexity
- Feature: Research-oriented answer engine
- Target user: Users who want fast answers with source-backed exploration
- Core JTBD: Help me get to a credible answer quickly without manually opening twenty tabs
- One-line PM take: Great speed-to-confidence product, but long-horizon synthesis and workflow continuity can improve further

## 2. Problem Being Solved
Research on the open web is slow, fragmented, and mentally expensive. Users want an answer engine that compresses discovery and synthesis while preserving trust through citations.

## 3. Primary User
Students, analysts, PMs, founders, and curious generalists who care more about speed plus references than browsing depth.

## 4. Current Experience
Users ask a question, receive a synthesized answer, and inspect supporting links. The main value is compressed research time and better orientation.

## 5. What Works
- Fast answer-first interaction
- Citation scaffolding creates trust
- Strong fit for exploratory and comparative questions

## 6. Friction / Gaps
- Source quality can vary widely
- Multi-step research projects need better persistence and synthesis
- Users can confuse citation presence with factual certainty

## 7. AI Product Risks
- Citation theater if source quality is weak
- False confidence from polished synthesis
- Retention may flatten if deep workflows are not supported

## 8. Metrics That Matter
- Activation: first successful cited query
- Engagement: research sessions per user per week
- Retention: repeat usage for knowledge tasks
- Quality: citation click-through + satisfaction
- Business: paid conversion among heavy researchers

## 9. Improvement Opportunities
| Priority | Opportunity | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| P0 | Research workspace with synthesis memory | Makes the tool usable for deeper ongoing projects |
| P1 | Source quality labeling | Helps users calibrate trust faster |
| P2 | PM-style summary export formats | Useful for teams turning research into decisions |

## 10. PM Recommendation
Double down on the path from answer engine to research operating system. The next moat is not just retrieval — it is structured synthesis over time.
